# Claiming GST on Business Expenses

You can claim GST (input tax) on goods and services you buy for your taxable activity. To claim GST you must:

1. **Hold a valid tax invoice** — for purchases over $50, you need a full tax invoice showing the supplier's name, GST number, date, description, and GST amount
2. **Use the goods/services in your taxable activity** — private use is not claimable
3. **Claim in the correct period** — the period the invoice was issued (invoice basis) or paid (payments basis)

## Mixed-use assets
If you use something for both business and private purposes, you can only claim the business portion. Common examples: home office, vehicle, phone.

## Adjustments
If the business use of an asset changes, you may need to make a GST adjustment. For example, if you claimed full GST on a vehicle but now use it 50% privately.

## Cannot claim GST on
- Entertainment expenses (50% deductible for income tax but no GST claim)
- Penalty or interest charges
- Donations
- Private purchases
- Purchases from non-GST-registered suppliers
