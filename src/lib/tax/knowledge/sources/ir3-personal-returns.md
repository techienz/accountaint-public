# IR3 — Personal Income Tax Return

## Overview

The IR3 is the personal income tax return for individuals who have income not fully taxed at source. Company shareholders typically need to file an IR3 to report shareholder salary, dividends, and any other income.

## Income sources for company shareholders

### Shareholder salary
- Declared by the company as an expense (deductible to company)
- Taxed as employment income at the shareholder's marginal rate
- PAYE should be deducted and paid to IRD
- Must be a reasonable amount for the work performed

### Dividends
- Paid from after-tax company profits
- Grossed-up for imputation credits: Gross dividend = Cash dividend / (1 - company tax rate)
- Imputation credits offset the personal tax liability
- Example: $72,000 cash dividend with full imputation = $100,000 gross, $28,000 imputation credits

### Other income
- **Rental income**: Net rental income after expenses (rates, insurance, mortgage interest, maintenance, depreciation)
- **Interest income**: Bank interest, with RWT already deducted
- **Other dividends**: From investments in other companies
- **Employment income**: If also employed elsewhere

## Personal tax rates (2025-26)

| Taxable income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $14,000 | 10.5% |
| $14,001 – $48,000 | 17.5% |
| $48,001 – $70,000 | 30% |
| $70,001 – $180,000 | 33% |
| $180,001+ | 39% |

## Tax credits
- Imputation credits (from company dividends)
- RWT credits (from interest income)
- PAYE credits (from employment income)
- Independent earner tax credit ($520/year if income between $24,000-$48,000)
- Donation tax credits (33.33% of donations to approved donees)

## Salary vs dividend optimisation

The total tax on company profits depends on how they're extracted:
- **All salary**: Deductible to company (no company tax), but taxed at shareholder's marginal rate
- **All dividend**: Company pays 28%, shareholder pays marginal rate less imputation credits
- **Optimal mix**: Usually a blend that keeps the shareholder's marginal rate low while using imputation credits efficiently

Key insight: If the shareholder's marginal rate is below 28%, salary is more tax-efficient. If above 33%, dividends with imputation credits may be more efficient. The optimal split depends on the total income picture.

## Filing deadline
- Standard: 7 July following the tax year end
- With tax agent: extension typically to 31 March of the following year
