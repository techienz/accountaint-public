# Zero-rated and Exempt Supplies

## Zero-rated supplies (0% GST)
These are taxable but at 0% — you can still claim input tax on related expenses:
- **Exports** — goods and services supplied to overseas customers
- **Sale of a going concern** — selling a business as a whole to a GST-registered buyer
- **Land transactions** — between GST-registered parties (since 2011)
- **Fine metals** — gold, silver, platinum

## Exempt supplies (no GST)
These are not taxable — you cannot claim input tax on related expenses:
- **Financial services** — interest, bank fees, insurance premiums, life insurance
- **Residential rent** — renting out a dwelling for residential use
- **Donated goods sold by non-profits**

## Impact on GST claims
If you make both taxable and exempt supplies, you can only claim input tax on expenses related to your taxable supplies. You may need to make apportionment adjustments.
