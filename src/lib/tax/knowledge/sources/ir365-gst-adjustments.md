# GST Adjustments and Corrections

## Credit and debit notes
- **Credit note** — issued when you reduce the price or the customer returns goods. Reduces GST previously accounted for.
- **Debit note** — issued when additional amounts are charged.

Both must contain specific information (supplier's name, GST number, date, reason for adjustment, amount of GST adjustment).

## Bad debts
If you have accounted for GST on a sale (invoice basis) and the debt becomes bad:
- You can claim a **bad debt deduction** for the GST component
- The debt must be written off in your accounts
- You must have made reasonable efforts to recover the debt

## Corrections
- **Errors under $1,000** — can be corrected in the next GST return
- **Errors over $1,000** — must file an amended return or make a voluntary disclosure
- Time limit: generally within **4 years** of the original return

## Change of use adjustments
If you change the proportion of business vs private use of an asset, you may need to make periodic GST adjustments to reflect the actual use.
