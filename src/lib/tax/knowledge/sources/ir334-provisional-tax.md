# Provisional Tax (IR334)

Provisional tax is income tax paid in instalments during the year, rather than in a lump sum at end of year. You must pay provisional tax if your residual income tax (RIT) is more than $5,000 ($2,500 for individuals).

## Methods

### Standard method
- Based on prior year's RIT plus 5% uplift
- Three instalments for March balance date:
  - **28 August** (P1)
  - **15 January** (P2)
  - **7 May** (P3)

### Estimation method
- You estimate your tax liability for the year
- Same three instalment dates as standard
- Useful if income is expected to be lower than last year
- Must re-estimate by the third instalment date

### AIM (Accounting Income Method)
- Pay based on actual income each period using approved accounting software
- Payments due on GST return dates (monthly or two-monthly)
- No use-of-money interest if using AIM correctly
- Available to businesses with turnover under $5 million

## Use-of-money interest (UOMI)
Interest is charged on underpayments and paid on overpayments. Current rates are published by IRD.
