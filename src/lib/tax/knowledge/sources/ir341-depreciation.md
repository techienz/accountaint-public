# Depreciation (IR341)

Business assets that lose value over time can be depreciated — you claim a deduction each year for the decline in value.

## Methods
- **Diminishing value (DV)** — depreciation calculated on the asset's adjusted tax value each year
- **Straight line (SL)** — equal amounts each year over the asset's useful life

You choose the method when you first claim depreciation on an asset. IRD publishes depreciation rates for common asset types.

## Low-value assets
Assets costing **$1,000 or less** (GST exclusive) can be fully expensed in the year of purchase — no need to depreciate.

## Buildings
Depreciation on buildings was removed in 2011–12 for buildings with an estimated useful life of 50 years or more. Some commercial fit-out and short-life buildings can still be depreciated.

## Disposal
When you sell or dispose of an asset:
- If sale price > adjusted tax value → **depreciation recovery** (income)
- If sale price < adjusted tax value → **loss on disposal** (deduction)
