# Record Keeping Requirements

All businesses must keep sufficient records to enable IRD to determine their tax obligations. Records must be kept for a minimum of **7 years**.

## Required records
- **Income records** — invoices issued, cash register tapes, bank statements
- **Expense records** — receipts, invoices received, credit card statements
- **Bank records** — all business bank statements and reconciliations
- **Asset records** — purchase price, depreciation schedules, disposal records
- **Employee records** — wages, PAYE deductions, KiwiSaver, employment agreements
- **GST records** — tax invoices (issued and received), GST return working papers

## Electronic records
Electronic records are acceptable provided they are:
- Readily accessible and retrievable
- Able to be produced in printed form if required
- Backed up regularly

## Penalties for poor records
IRD can impose penalties if records are inadequate:
- **$250** per return period for failure to keep records
- Increased risk of IRD default assessments
- Potential shortfall penalties if tax is underpaid due to poor records
