# IR4 — Company Income Tax Return

## Overview

The IR4 is the income tax return for New Zealand companies. It reports the company's taxable income and calculates the tax payable at the company tax rate (currently 28%).

## Key steps in preparation

### 1. Start with accounting profit
Use the Profit & Loss statement from Xero as the starting point. This gives you net profit before tax adjustments.

### 2. Identify add-backs (non-deductible expenses)
Certain expenses shown in the P&L are not deductible for tax purposes and must be added back:

- **Entertainment (50%)**: Only 50% of entertainment expenses are deductible (s DD 1). Common examples: meals with clients, staff social events, corporate hospitality.
- **Penalties and fines**: Government-imposed penalties are never deductible (s DA 2(2)).
- **Donations**: Not deductible as an expense — claim as a tax credit on the shareholder's IR3 instead.
- **Personal expenses**: Any expenses that are private or domestic in nature.
- **Drawings**: Owner drawings charged as expenses must be removed.
- **Depreciation**: Accounting depreciation is replaced with tax depreciation (may differ).
- **Life insurance**: Premiums on policies for shareholders/directors are generally non-deductible.

### 3. Calculate tax depreciation
Tax depreciation may differ from accounting depreciation. Use IRD-approved rates (IR265) and either:
- Diminishing Value (DV) method
- Straight Line (SL) method

### 4. Claim deductions
Additional tax deductions that may not appear in accounting records:
- Home office claim (proportional or sqm rate method)
- Motor vehicle expenses (mileage rate or actual cost method)
- Tax depreciation (replaces accounting depreciation)

### 5. Loss carry-forward
If the company has a tax loss, it can be carried forward to offset future income, provided:
- Shareholder continuity is maintained (49% commonality rule)
- The loss is documented and tracked year-on-year

### 6. Calculate tax payable
Taxable income × 28% = income tax payable.
Less: provisional tax already paid, RWT credits, imputation credits.

## Filing deadline
- Standard balance date (31 March): return due 7 July (with extension to 31 March following year if using a tax agent)

## Common issues
- Forgetting to add back entertainment expenses
- Using accounting depreciation instead of tax depreciation
- Not claiming home office or vehicle deductions
- Not reconciling shareholder current accounts before filing
