# Fringe Benefit Tax (IR409)

Fringe benefit tax (FBT) applies when an employer provides non-cash benefits to employees. The most common fringe benefit is a motor vehicle available for private use.

## Common fringe benefits
- **Motor vehicles** — if available for private use by employees
- **Low-interest loans** — below market interest rate
- **Free or subsidised goods/services**
- **Employer contributions to insurance, funds, etc.**

## FBT rates
- **Single rate**: 63.93% (if you don't know employees' marginal tax rates)
- **Alternate rate**: varies based on employee's cash remuneration bracket
- **Pooled alternate rate**: weighted average for all employees

## Filing and payment
- **Quarterly** returns due on the 20th of the month following each quarter:
  - Q1 (Apr-Jun) → 20 July
  - Q2 (Jul-Sep) → 20 October
  - Q3 (Oct-Dec) → 20 January
  - Q4 (Jan-Mar) → 31 May (annual adjustment)
- **Annual** filing option available for small employers

## Motor vehicle FBT
The most common benefit. Calculated on the tax-inclusive cost of the vehicle. Reduced if the vehicle is not available for private use (e.g., logbook kept showing business-only use for certain days).
