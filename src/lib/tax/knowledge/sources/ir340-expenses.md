# Business Expenses (IR340)

You can deduct expenses from your business income if they are incurred in earning assessable income. The expense must be a genuine business cost, not capital in nature (unless depreciation applies).

## Common deductible expenses
- **Accounting and legal fees** — fully deductible if business-related
- **Vehicle expenses** — actual costs or IRD mileage rate (currently 99c/km for first 14,000km)
- **Home office** — proportion based on floor area or number of rooms used for business
- **Phone and internet** — business-use proportion
- **Insurance** — business insurance premiums
- **Stationery and consumables**
- **Training and professional development** — if related to current business
- **Bad debts** — debts previously included as income and genuinely written off

## Non-deductible expenses
- Capital expenditure (claim depreciation instead)
- Fines and penalties
- 50% of entertainment expenses
- Private or domestic expenses
- Tax penalties and interest (UOMI interest is deductible)

## Records required
Keep receipts, invoices, and records for **7 years** minimum.
