# PAYE for Employers (IR345)

If you have employees, you must deduct PAYE (Pay As You Earn) from their wages and salary and pay it to IRD.

## Employer obligations
1. **Register as an employer** with IRD
2. **Deduct PAYE** from each pay based on the employee's tax code
3. **File employment information** (payday filing) — within 2 working days of each payday
4. **Pay deductions to IRD**:
   - Monthly payer: by the **20th of the following month**
   - Twice-monthly payer: by the **5th and 20th** of the following month

## KiwiSaver
- Employees can contribute 3%, 4%, 6%, 8%, or 10% of gross pay
- Employer must contribute a minimum of 3%
- ESCT (Employer Superannuation Contribution Tax) is deducted from the employer contribution

## Schedular payments
Payments to contractors may require withholding tax at a specified rate. Common schedular activities include contract workers, IT contractors, and consultants.

## Important: twice-monthly threshold
You must pay twice-monthly if your total PAYE and ESCT deductions are $500,000 or more per year.
