# ACC Levies

## Overview

ACC (Accident Compensation Corporation) levies fund New Zealand's no-fault accident insurance scheme. Businesses and self-employed individuals pay levies based on their liable earnings and industry risk classification.

## Levy components

### 1. Work levy
- Paid by businesses based on their Classification Unit (CU) code
- Rate varies by industry risk (higher risk = higher levy)
- Applied per $100 of liable earnings

### 2. Earner levy
- Paid by individuals on their earnings
- Flat rate across all industries
- Deducted from salary/wages via PAYE, or invoiced to self-employed

### 3. Working Safer levy
- Small additional levy to fund workplace health and safety initiatives
- Applied per $100 of liable earnings

## Classification Units (CU codes)

Each business is assigned a CU code based on its primary activity:
- **72610**: Computer services, IT consulting
- **69100**: Accounting and tax services
- **73100**: Architectural and engineering services
- **41100**: Residential building construction
- **45210**: Electrical services

The CU code determines the work levy rate. Businesses should check their CU code is correct — an incorrect code can mean paying too much or too little.

## Liable earnings

### For self-employed / shareholder-employees
- Based on earnings as a self-employed person or shareholder-employee
- Minimum liable earnings: approximately $35,000/year (updated annually)
- Maximum liable earnings cap: approximately $139,384/year (updated annually)

### For employees
- Based on gross earnings
- Employer pays the work levy on employee earnings
- Employee pays the earner levy (deducted via PAYE)

## Shareholder-employee salary
- Company pays work levy on shareholder-employee salaries
- The shareholder-employee's salary should be declared in the company's IR4 and their personal IR3
- ACC levies are a deductible business expense

## Annual invoice
- ACC sends an annual invoice based on the previous year's return
- Compare the invoice against your own calculation to check for errors
- Common issues: wrong CU code, incorrect liable earnings figure, not accounting for the earnings cap

## Tips for review
1. Verify your CU code matches your actual business activity
2. Check liable earnings match what was reported to IRD
3. Ensure the earnings cap has been applied correctly
4. Compare year-on-year to identify any unusual changes
5. ACC levies are a deductible business expense — claim them in your IR4
